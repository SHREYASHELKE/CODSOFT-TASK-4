Abdul kalam

scientist | teacher | visionary | author | president of india

About The Kalam

  • Born on October 15, 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu

  • Studied physics and aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology

  • Worked at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO)

  • Developed India's first indigenous satellitelaunch vehicle (SLV-III) and the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles

  • Received the Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, Indira Gandhi Award of National Integration, Veer Savarkar Award, Ramanujan Award, King Charles II Medal, and Hoover Medal

  • Served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007

  • Known as the "Missile Man of India"and "People's President"

  • Kalam was a key figure in India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. He played a significant role in the Pokhran-I nuclear tests in 1998.

  • Kalam's vision was to develop India into a developed country by 2020. He proposed the Technology Vision 2020 plan to achieve this goal.

  • Kalam was a visiting professor at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) in Ahmedabad, IIM Shillong, and IIM-Indore after his presidency.
